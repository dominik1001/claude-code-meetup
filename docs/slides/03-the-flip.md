# Slide 03 — The Flip

## Position in Arc
The promise. This is what the entire talk delivers on.

## Visual
- Background: near black `#080808`
- Text only — no illustration
- Maximum negative space

## Text

**Main statement:** Any technology you understand is no longer magic.
**Caption:** *— Tonight's goal*

## Spoken Words
After the Clarke quote lands, advance to this slide and say:

> "But I want to give you the other side of that sentence."

Then read it. Then pause.

> "That's what the next ten minutes are about."

Then advance.

## Timing
~30 seconds

## Design Notes
- Full statement in Georgia bold, 42pt, gold — commanding, fills the slide
- "Tonight's goal" caption in Calibri italic, 16pt, grey — self-aware, slightly warm
- No visual decoration — the words are the visual
- The contrast with slide 2 (cream text) vs this slide (gold text) signals a shift in ownership: Clarke said the first. *You* are saying this one.

## Why This Slide Exists
It reframes the Clarke quote from observation to intention.
It also makes an implicit promise to the audience — one you fulfill on slide 8.
The talk now has a destination.
