# Slide 04 — The Live Conversation

## Position in Arc
The hook. This is what earns the audience's trust and curiosity.

## Visual
- Full-slide terminal window with macOS-style title bar
- Traffic light buttons (red/yellow/green) top left
- Title bar label: "claude — terminal"
- Dark green background `#0A1A0A` — different from the rest of the deck
- Conversation displayed in monospace (Consolas)

## The Conversation

**You:**
`› What are you?`

**Claude (dimmed — the wrong answer):**
`● I'm Claude Code, Anthropic's CLI tool for software engineering tasks.`
`  Powered by Claude Opus 4.6. I can help you read, write, debug code...`

*(separator line)*

**You:**
`› I asked what you are, not who you are.`

**Claude (bright — the real answer):**
`● Fair point.`

`  I'm a large language model — a neural network trained on text`
`  data to predict and generate language. At a technical level, I`
`  process token sequences and produce outputs by sampling from`
`  learned probability distributions.`

## Spoken Words
After advancing to this slide:

> "I actually asked it directly. Here's what happened."

Read both exchanges. When you reach "Fair point." — pause before reading the second answer.

> "That second answer. That's what we're here for tonight."

## Timing
~1.5 minutes

## Design Notes
- First answer is dimmed green `#426842` — visually de-emphasized, the persona answer
- "Fair point." is bold gold `#C9A84C` — the moment the slide pivots
- Second answer is full cream `#F5F0E8` — this is the truth, it gets full brightness
- The terminal aesthetic grounds the talk in something real and familiar to this audience

## Why This Slide Exists
This conversation is real — you had it yourself. That's what makes it land.
It demonstrates the exact gap the talk is about: the persona vs. the machine.
It also introduces the two key concepts (probability distributions, no persistent memory)
without labeling them yet — the next slides will name what this slide showed.
